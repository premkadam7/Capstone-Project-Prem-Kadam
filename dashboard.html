Reflection & Conclusions
Model performance analysis · Strategic learnings · Areas of improvement
Q1 · Did predictions align with actual market movement?
Partially. INFY and HINDUNILVR moved in the predicted direction. HDFCBANK closed slightly lower than the Day 2 forecast but the directional call was correct over the two-day window. SUNPHARMA pulled back while MARUTI fell short of the ensemble's optimistic Day 2 target. Ensemble MAPE on actual trading day prices ranged from ~0.5% to 2%, broadly consistent with Dataset A evaluation performance.
Q2 · Which model performed best and why?
LSTM achieved the lowest average MAPE (2.39%) across all five stocks during the evaluation period, significantly outperforming ARIMA (9.29%) and Prophet (9.03%). LSTM captures non-linear patterns in price sequences that simpler linear models miss. Prophet had a slight edge over ARIMA on directional accuracy (51.5% vs 22.0%) due to its trend-plus-seasonality structure.
Q3 · Was GARCH volatility estimation useful?
Yes. GARCH correctly ranked HINDUNILVR and SUNPHARMA as lower-volatility stocks relative to MARUTI and INFY. Strategy B's inverse-vol weighting gave more capital to defensive names, partially offsetting the MARUTI overweight from Strategy A. Without this component, the portfolio would have had higher MARUTI concentration and greater overall risk.
Q4 · What would be done differently?
Key improvements: (1) Include volume, sector index returns, and short-term momentum as LSTM inputs — pure price-based LSTM tends to lag actual moves. (2) Use walk-forward validation instead of a single train-test split for more reliable out-of-sample estimates. (3) Incorporate regime detection to adapt model weights dynamically based on market state.
